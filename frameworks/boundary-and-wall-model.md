# Boundary & Wall Model™

**Source:** Chapters 6–7  
**Type:** Structural Metaphor / Behavioral Framework

## Walls vs. Boundaries
| Feature | Walls | Boundaries |
|---------|-------|------------|
| Permeability | Non-permeable (blocks everything) | Permeable (filters, protects, allows) |
| Function | Hides pain, blocks connection, traps the self | Delineates responsibility, protects energy, enables flow |
| Outcome | Compartmentalization, impostor syndrome, emotional leakage | Clarity, self-respect, unconditional alignment |

## Canonical Principle
> *"Walls stop some bad from getting in, but they also stop the good from getting in... Boundaries are limits and standards for what you will and will not allow."* (Ch 6)
